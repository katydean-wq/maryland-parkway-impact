Maryland Parkway construction impact — where we stand and what happens when it ends

Construction is responsible for a much larger share of the Maryland Parkway sales decline than the earlier draft suggested. There was no pre-existing secular decline at the plaza; foot traffic was stable through July 2024 and crashed 35% within four months of construction starting. The recovery question is now sharper: how much of the lost plaza traffic returns when construction ends, and how quickly can MP rebuild its capture rate?

What changed in v3. With full weekly Placer data for 2023, 2024, 2025, and 2026 (through May 18), the decomposition story has changed significantly. Plaza visits did NOT have a pre-existing secular decline — they were stable at ~23-25k/wk through July 2024, then dropped sharply when construction began. There was no secular decline. Construction caused the plaza traffic loss.

Earlier drafts framed plaza traffic as having a pre-existing 17%/year decline. That came from annual totals, which mixed 2024's stable first half with its construction-impacted second half. With weekly data we can see what actually happened.

Monthly average weekly plaza visits by year. 2023 was completely stable at 22-25k/wk. 2024 held the same level through July, then crashed in August - October. 2025 and 2026 stabilized at the lower equilibrium.
Construction started in summer 2024 and the plaza lost ~10k weekly visits within four months — from 21.8k in July 2024 to 13.8k in October 2024. The drop is sharp, sustained, and not present in 2023. This is direct construction impact on the area, not a secular trend.

MP weathered it through early 2025 — then the dam broke

Despite the plaza losing 35% of its foot traffic in late 2024, MP sales held at $30-33k/week through the end of 2024 and into Q1 2025. MP was retaining customers even as the area lost visitors. Then in May 2025, when work intensified near the venue, MP sales started declining.

MP weekly sales (left axis) vs plaza visits (right axis). Note how MP holds steady through fall/winter 2024 even as plaza visits crash. The MP capture rate actually rose during this period.

MP was nearly flat YoY through May 2025 (−0.4%), even while mature peers were up +6.2%. MP underperformance pre-construction-breakpoint was only ~6 pp. The big MP-specific damage came in late 2025 and is now compounded by a brand-wide drop in 2026.

The construction → sales correlation

Each point below is a single week from August 2024 through May 2026. X-axis is cumulative construction-intensity for the venue's segment. Y-axis is MP underperformance vs. the mature peer benchmark.

Cumulative construction intensity has a strong, statistically significant negative relationship with the MP-vs-benchmark gap (r = −0.41, r² = 0.17, p < 0.001, n = 95 weeks).
Current-week intensity barely matters. Raw weekly correlation is only r = −0.07 (not significant). Once people stop coming, this week's specific disruption is irrelevant; they were already gone.
Cumulative exposure matters a lot. Every week of construction deepens the gap. The damage compounds and doesn't reset when work moves elsewhere along the corridor.

The 2026 brand-wide drop is real and unexplained

Every mature Escapology location dropped 35–46% in YTD-2026 vs YTD-2025 (same ISO-week window). This is not seasonality, not vintage, not Maryland-Parkway-specific. Something hit the brand company-wide in early 2026.

YTD through ISO week 21, weekly average, by mature location.
This is the open question for the exec team. Construction explains MP's underperformance vs. peers through 2025 (peak gap −42 pp in Q4 2025). The 2026 drop in MP is partly construction-continued and partly brand-wide. Without identifying what hit the brand in Q1 2026, we cannot cleanly separate the two — and the recovery scenarios that follow depend on this. Candidates: POS/reporting change, group-sales channel shift, brand-wide pricing change, recessionary effect on discretionary entertainment.

Methodology & data sources

Inputs

  • Ops Reporting workbooks 2024–2026 — daily POS by location
  • Placer.ai weekly visits 2023–2026 (through May 18) to Sunrise City Plaza
  • 96 RTC Maryland Parkway weekly PDFs (Aug 2024 – May 2026), OCR'd via tesseract; 462 events extracted
  • GA4, Search Console, Google Ads via Windsor.ai

Method notes (v3)

  • Mature benchmark: DES, FTL, ORL, TS (all opened ≥ 6 years before our baseline)
  • YTD comparisons use ISO weeks 1–21 — same window each year to handle incomplete 2026 + moving holidays
  • Construction intensity: severity × work-type × exp(−distance/1 mi), summed weekly
  • Cumulative intensity since project start (Aug 2024) is the strongest correlate of MP underperformance (r = −0.41, p < 0.001)
  • Capture rate: weekly MP sales ÷ weekly plaza visits × 1000

Caveats

  • 2026 sales data goes through May 30; Placer data through May 18. Q2 2026 is incomplete.
  • The brand-wide 2026 drop is undiagnosed — without identifying its cause, recovery scenarios are illustrative not predictive.
  • OCR adds noise to severity/work-type classification of construction events.
  • MP and Town Square share several Las Vegas Google Ads campaigns; MP-attributable spend uses a 50% allocation of shared spend.
