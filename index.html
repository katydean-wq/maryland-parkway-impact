Maryland Parkway construction impact — where we stand and what happens when it ends

Construction on the RTC's Maryland Parkway BRT project is a real factor in our 2025–2026 sales loss at the Maryland Parkway venue — but it explains less of the decline than it might appear, and we should not expect a full recovery when construction concludes in Fall 2026.

Bottom line. Plaza foot traffic is roughly stable — customers are still in the area. The dramatic sales drop is driven by collapsing capture rate: MP captures 57% fewer dollars per plaza visitor than at the height of construction summer 2025. This is a brand/awareness/consideration problem at least as much as it is an access problem, and the trend is still deepening. Even an optimistic post-construction recovery projects sales settling around $21k/wk — still 29% below pre-construction baseline.

Weekly sales: MP vs. peer benchmark

Our peer benchmark — the indexed average of 8 long-running locations (DES, FTL, NR, ORL, PF, ST, SW, TS) — controls for brand-wide seasonality, marketing changes, and post-COVID recovery patterns. MP tracked the benchmark almost exactly through May 2025, then diverged sharply and has stayed below.

Structural break detected at week of May 19, 2025 by single change-point analysis. Pre-break MP-vs-benchmark gap: +0.5%. Post-break: −32%. This date corresponds to the RTC's "Adjacent Project Marathon Closure" at Tropicana / Maryland Pkwy that same week.

The smoking gun: capture rate collapse

If construction simply reduced foot traffic, we'd expect sales and visits to fall together. They didn't. Plaza visits have been roughly flat at 14–15k/week throughout the construction period. MP sales-per-1,000-plaza-visits has fallen 57% — meaning a smaller and smaller share of the people coming to the plaza are choosing Escapology.

MP sales (left axis, $/week) vs. plaza visits (right axis, count/week). Sales fall and stay low; visits do not.
$ MP sales per 1,000 plaza visitors. The capture rate has fallen progressively, with the steepest drop in Q1–Q2 2026 as construction work moved closer to the venue.
Search Console impressions for the MP location pages have collapsed 83% from Q1 2025 to Q2 2026 — people are searching for us less often.
This is the central finding. The plaza is still drawing customers, but they are not coming to Escapology. Combined with the 83% drop in search impressions and a steep decline in GA4 sessions, this looks less like a "can't physically get there" problem and more like a "forgot about it / stopped considering it" problem. Construction may have triggered it, but the disengagement is now its own dynamic.

What happens when construction ends?

Construction is currently estimated to wrap Fall 2026 (per RTC's published schedule, subject to change). Three scenarios for what MP sales look like once it does:

Even our optimistic case projects MP at 71% of pre-construction. The reasons it can't fully snap back:
  • Secular plaza decline. Sunrise City Plaza was losing 17%/yr before construction started. That trend doesn't pause and won't reverse.
  • Brand awareness erosion. Search impressions for MP location pages are down 83%. Even if construction ends, rebuilding consideration takes intentional marketing investment over many months.
  • Behavioral inertia. Customers who stopped visiting during construction develop alternative habits. National data on construction recovery shows partial recapture, rarely full.
What could move us toward the optimistic case.
  • Targeted re-engagement campaign timed to construction completion (paid + organic).
  • Repeat-customer winback (the 2024 customer file — they're the easiest to bring back).
  • UNLV partnership / student promo — the area is anchored by UNLV; a deliberate campus push exploits the captive audience.
  • Visible signage and "we're open" messaging starting before construction ends (during the wind-down phase).

Methodology & data sources

Inputs

  • Ops Reporting workbooks 2024, 2025, 2026 (daily POS by location)
  • Placer.ai weekly visits to Sunrise City Plaza, plus 2023–2025 annual totals
  • 96 RTC Maryland Parkway weekly construction PDFs (Aug 2024 – May 2026), OCR'd in-sandbox via tesseract
  • Google Analytics 4 — MP page-path traffic via Windsor.ai
  • Google Search Console — MP page queries, impressions, clicks via Windsor.ai
  • Google Ads — Las Vegas account (MP-specific, Town Square, and shared Las Vegas campaigns)

Method notes

  • Benchmark: indexed (vs. each location's 2024 mean) average of DES, FTL, NR, ORL, PF, ST, SW, TS.
  • Construction start detected via single change-point analysis on MP-vs-benchmark weekly index.
  • Intensity score: severity × work-type × exp(−distance/1 mi), summed over events active each week.
  • Capture rate = weekly MP sales ÷ weekly plaza visits × 1000.
  • Windsor.ai 16-month data window limits Google data to Feb 2025 onward.
  • Recovery scenarios assume Fall 2026 completion and use Q1–Q2 2026 plaza visits as the post-construction baseline.

Caveats

  • OCR adds noise; the per-event severity classification is approximate.
  • The benchmark assumes peer locations had no idiosyncratic shocks in 2025–2026. Spot-checks indicate they are largely flat.
  • Maryland Parkway and Town Square share several Las Vegas Google Ads campaigns. The MP-attributable spend uses a 50% allocation of shared spend.
  • Google My Business connector has no Maryland Parkway location — call/direction signals are not yet incorporated.
