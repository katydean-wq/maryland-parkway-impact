Two observations worth flagging for the exec team:
  1. MP was already down 28% from 2023 to 2024 H1 — that's BEFORE construction started in August 2024. There is a pre-existing MP-specific issue we have not yet explained. Worth investigating: operational changes at the location, local competitor openings, demographic shifts, hours changes, etc.
  2. Construction drag is now visible and compounding: MP held flat from 2024 to 2025 while peers grew +12% (≈ 12 pp construction drag). Then dropped 26% from 2025 to 2026 while peers grew +12% (≈ 38 pp construction drag). The longer construction goes on, the worse it gets.

The construction → sales correlation

Each point below is a single week from August 2024 through May 2026. X-axis is cumulative construction-intensity. Y-axis is MP underperformance vs. the mature peer benchmark (which we now know is growing in 2026, making the gap larger than a naive comparison would suggest).

Cumulative construction intensity has a strong, statistically significant negative relationship with the MP-vs-peer gap. r = −0.54, r² = 0.29, p < 0.001 (n = 95 weeks).
Cumulative exposure explains 29% of the gap variance. That's a meaningful correlation for a single explanatory variable, and consistent with construction being the primary driver. The remaining variance is attributable to local marketing, web/SEO, capture-rate dynamics, and noise.
Current-week intensity barely matters (r = −0.04). The damage is from the accumulated experience, not this week's specific work. This is what behavioral fatigue and brand-awareness erosion look like.

Recovery scenarios

The question for the exec team is: when construction ends, how much of the lost plaza traffic returns, and how quickly does MP rebuild its capture rate? Peers are growing, suggesting demand for the brand exists.

The optimistic case is plausible — peer locations are growing. If construction was the primary driver of plaza traffic loss (the data strongly supports this) and the brand has organic demand growth (peers are up +12% YoY in 2026), MP could realistically return to its pre-construction trajectory after construction ends.
The pre-existing 2023→2024 MP decline is the open question. If that decline reflects a structural local-market change (demographic shift, new competition, anchor tenant loss), some portion of the loss may be permanent regardless of construction outcome.

Methodology & data sources

Inputs

  • Ops Reporting workbooks 2024, 2025, 2026 — weekly totals from row 156 of each Week sheet
  • 2023 weekly sales provided directly for MP, DES, FTL, ORL, PF, TS
  • Placer.ai weekly visits 2023–2026 to Sunrise City Plaza
  • 96 RTC Maryland Parkway weekly PDFs (Aug 2024 – May 2026), OCR'd via tesseract; 462 events
  • GA4, Search Console, Google Ads via Windsor.ai (Feb 2025 onward due to 16-month limit)

Method notes

  • Authoritative weekly totals: row 156 of each Week sheet (Excel 1-indexed)
  • Mature benchmark: DES, FTL, ORL, PF, TS (vintage 2017-2022)
  • YTD comparisons use ISO weeks 1-21 — same window each year (2026 data through week 21)
  • Intensity score: severity × work-type × exp(−distance/1 mi), summed weekly
  • Cumulative intensity since project start (Aug 2024) is strongest predictor (r = −0.54)
  • Capture rate: weekly MP sales ÷ weekly plaza visits × 1000

Caveats

  • 2026 data is partial (through week 21 = May 24). Q2 2026 is incomplete.
  • The 2023→2024 MP decline (−28%) is unexplained and predates construction.
  • OCR adds noise to construction event classification.
  • Google My Business has no Maryland Parkway location connected — call signals not incorporated.
